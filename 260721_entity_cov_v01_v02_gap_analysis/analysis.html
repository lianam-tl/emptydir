Why the entity-cov v0.1 gain disappears in v0.2

Controlled comparison of consol-h0mn2x step 400 → 2000. Both sides use GPT‑5.2 entity matching; only the output task and segment construction differ.

Verdict: v0.1 did not show a broad 10-minute improvement. 86.8% of its +4.00 pp gain comes from the single 3-minute sport-01 sample. Without that clip, the gain is +0.57 pp, with a video-cluster bootstrap interval crossing zero. The best-supported mechanism is that v0.2 derives coverage from exhaustive shot metadata, so the same sport clip no longer has the flat span-extraction bottleneck seen at step 400.

3. “10 minutes == half” is not true for these datasets

v0.1 chunk_10m

20 rows, mean 7.2 min, range 0.2–10.0 min.

6 rows are under five minutes because the fixed 10-minute cutter retains tiny final remainders.

v0.2 half

13 rows, mean 11.1 min, range 3.0–16.1 min.

Each film is split into two equal halves; the 3-minute sport video remains one row.

Both views cover the same 144.4 minutes of source video, but they create different character observations: v0.1 has 97, while v0.2 half has 77. The evaluator averages per-character IoUs, so boundaries and repeated characters change the weighting.

Long-chunk check: keeping only v0.1 rows at least five minutes long reduces the s400 → s2000 gain from +4.00 pp to +1.17 pp.

4. What actually improved in v0.1: explicit span exhaustiveness on sport-01

sport-01 is the same whole 180-second source clip in both datasets, so this outlier is not a chunk-boundary difference. In v0.1, step 400 emitted 11 explicit appearance spans while step 2000 emitted 40, and its score jumped from 14.03% to 62.13%. In v0.2, coverage is reconstructed from shot_metadata[*].entities; both checkpoints produce 30 derived sport spans and nearly identical scores (38.34% vs 37.64%). This is direct evidence for a schema/task interaction, although one inference per checkpoint cannot prove the mechanism by itself.

v0.1 flat output

The model directly emits roster + spans. Across all rows, predicted spans rise from 1,350 to 1,804 (+33.6%). Step 2000’s sport result is much more exhaustive.

v0.2 nested output

The evaluator ignores non-person roster items and converts each shot-level person/character mention into a span. Across half rows, derived spans change from 1,788 to 1,459 (-18.4%). This is a different behavior from direct span extraction.

5. Remaining measurement noise

  • Only seven independent source videos are present. The 20 or 13 rows are cuts of those same videos, not 20 or 13 independent examples.
  • v0.2’s nested response is much larger. At step 400, 3/20 outputs hit the 65,536-token cap; at step 2000, 2/20 did. Both have the same penalized film-04 half row, so it does not explain the s400/s2000 reversal, but it adds noise.
  • GPT-5.2 rescoring confirms the missing gain, so GPT-5.4-mini matcher behavior is not the root cause.

Conclusion

The safest statement is: v0.1 provided weak evidence that later training improves explicit temporal-span extraction, dominated by one short sports clip. It did not establish a general entity-coverage improvement. v0.2 half tests a different output behavior—entity mentions inside exhaustive shot metadata—on different chunk boundaries, and therefore does not reproduce that single flat-span outlier.
